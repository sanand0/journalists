## Tamil Nadu Performance

**Headline**
**Tamil Nadu Wants to Be More Than India’s Factory. It Wants to Be the Lab Too**

**Deck**
The state’s performance story is no longer just assembly lines and exports. It is an attempt to move up from making products to owning more of the design, components and ideas behind them. ([Info & PR Dept.][4])

**Intro**
Tamil Nadu has already built the factory story. It is India’s top electronics-exporting state, with **$14.65 billion** of electronics exports in **2024-25**, and it accounted for about **33%** of India’s electronics exports. Now it is trying to deepen that advantage through the **Tamil Nadu Electronics Components Manufacturing Scheme**, which targets **₹30,000 crore** of investment and **60,000 jobs**. ([Info & PR Dept.][5])

**Subhead**: **The New Bet Is Components, Not Just Assembly**

That matters because assembly can scale fast but capture limited value. Components, semiconductor-linked manufacturing and design capabilities sit higher up the chain. Tamil Nadu’s new scheme is explicitly built to attract firms under the Centre’s components push, while the state’s semiconductor and advanced electronics policy is trying to pull more value-added manufacturing into Tamil Nadu rather than let it stop at final assembly. ([Info & PR Dept.][4])

**Subhead**: **The Lab Story Is Becoming Real**

This is not just a slogan. Tamil Nadu accounted for **23%** of all patent filings in India in **2024-25**, with **15,440 applications**, up **62%** from the previous year. That gives the state something few Indian manufacturing hubs can claim at once: scale in exports, depth in production, and a rapidly rising innovation pipeline. ([The Times of India][6])

**Subhead**: **The Unfinished Part Is the Quality of Work**

That is where the election-performance test gets tougher. Tamil Nadu’s own budget speech says the state has **41% of all women working in factories in India**. But NITI Aayog’s state macro report shows female labour-force participation is much higher in rural Tamil Nadu than in urban Tamil Nadu, and a large share of women workers remain self-employed rather than salaried. The state has cracked industrial scale. The next question is whether it can convert that into more formal, higher-value work at mass scale. ([Info & PR Dept.][7])

**Proof box**
**5 numbers that make the case**

- **$14.65 billion** electronics exports in 2024-25. ([Info & PR Dept.][5])
- **33%** of India’s electronics exports came from Tamil Nadu. ([Info & PR Dept.][8])
- **₹30,000 crore** targeted investment under the state’s components scheme. ([Info & PR Dept.][4])
- **60,000 jobs** targeted under that scheme. ([Info & PR Dept.][4])
- **15,440 patents**, or **23%** of India’s filings, in 2024-25. ([The Times of India][6])
