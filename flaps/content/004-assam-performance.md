## Assam Performance

**Headline**
**Assam’s Real Opposition Is on the Flood Map**

**Deck**
The state has a growth story and a poverty-reduction story. But every monsoon still taxes both of them. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])

**Intro**
Assam’s performance case is stronger than outsiders often assume. The **2025-26 budget** projects current-price GSDP growth of about **15%**, and the state aims for a **revenue balance** rather than a revenue deficit. NITI Aayog’s multidimensional poverty review shows Assam’s poverty headcount falling from **36.97%** to **14.47%** between the two survey periods it compares. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])

**Subhead**: **That Is the Progress Story**

This is not trivial. A state once seen mainly through insurgency, underdevelopment and lagging social indicators now has a much stronger poverty and public-finance story than its old reputation suggests. That is why the usual election frame — identity, immigration, law and order — misses part of what voters are really judging. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])

**Subhead**: **But Geography Still Has Veto Power**

The harder truth is that Assam’s deepest opposition is not a party. It is water. The NRSC/ISRO flood hazard atlas for Assam estimates a cumulative flood-affected area of **27.08 lakh hectares** between **1998 and 2023**. Of that, around **18.22 lakh hectares** is cropland exposed to flood hazard. In the **first flood wave of 2025** alone, **26 districts**, **2,630 villages** and more than **1.04 lakh people** were affected. ([National Road Safety Council][13])

**Subhead**: **That Is Why the Election Test Is Simple**

Has Assam developed enough to outrun its geography? Not in headlines. In fields, roads, crop losses, damaged homes and disrupted school calendars. Assam’s progress is real. But so is the annual geography tax that can erase part of it in weeks. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])

**Proof box**
**5 numbers that make the case**

- **15%** projected current-price GSDP growth in 2025-26. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])
- **Revenue balance** targeted in 2025-26. ([PRS Legislative Research][12])
- **36.97% → 14.47%** multidimensional poverty decline in Assam across NITI’s comparison periods. ([NITI AAYOG][14])
- **27.08 lakh hectares** cumulatively affected by flooding from 1998 to 2023. ([National Road Safety Council][13])
- **26 districts, 2,630 villages, 1.04 lakh people** affected in the first 2025 flood wave. ([Directorate of Economics and Statistics][15])

**Chart suggestions**
Open with a **double-axis contrast**: falling poverty on one side, recurring flood exposure on the other. Then use a **map of Assam’s flood hazard footprint** keyed to the NRSC atlas. A third graphic can show the flap’s whole argument in one line: **Growth line up, flood line recurring**. ([NITI AAYOG][14])
