## West Bengal Performance

**Headline**
**Bengal Is Asking Voters to Judge the Household Wallet, Not the Factory Gate**

**Deck**
The state’s most visible performance story is no longer industrial revival. It is direct cash, health cover and household support at massive scale. ([West Bengal Finance Department][1])

**Intro**
West Bengal’s 2026-27 interim budget makes the governing model hard to miss. Lakshmir Bhandar now reaches **2.21 crore women**, with an **annual allocation of ₹26,700 crore** and **cumulative spending above ₹74,000 crore**. On top of that, the state has rolled out Banglar Yuva Sathi, a **₹1,500-a-month** allowance for unemployed youth, and early registrations crossed **2 lakh** in a single day. ([West Bengal Finance Department][1])

**Subhead**: **The State Is Governing Through the Family Budget**

This is the core shift. Bengal is no longer easiest to read through the old lens of “industry first” versus “welfare first.” It is using the household budget itself as the main instrument of politics: women’s cash support, youth income support, farm relief, and a state-backed health shield that has crossed **1 crore hospitalisations**, with **₹13,156 crore** in cashless treatment and coverage of **8.5 crore-plus** residents. ([West Bengal Finance Department][1])

**Subhead**: **The Question Is Whether This Can Substitute for Faster Growth**

That is where the flap bites. West Bengal’s own budget speech says GSDP has risen nearly five-fold since 2010-11, but the more immediate comparison is less flattering: NSDP growth in **2024-25 was 9.86%**, below **Tamil Nadu’s 15.76%** and **Maharashtra’s 11.85%**. Bengal is not collapsing. But it is asking voters to judge the state less by factory take-off than by whether family finances feel steadier. ([West Bengal Finance Department][1])

**Subhead**: **That Makes This Election More Modern Than It Looks**

The standard election frame in Bengal is identity, polarisation, and party machinery. The deeper one is simpler: if the household wallet is protected, do voters care enough about the missing factory comeback to punish the government? That is the real performance referendum. ([West Bengal Finance Department][1])
