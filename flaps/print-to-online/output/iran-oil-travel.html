Oil To Travel: Pain Points For India Amid War In Gulf

As the region contends with the fallout of the Iran conflict, India is bracing for impact. At stake: energy security, market stability, and the safety of the large Indian diaspora

By Saurabh Sinha

What's happening on the ground

Airfares Zoom, Indians Stranded

  • Dubai and Doha are the two biggest choke-points for India-linked international travel. Emirates temporarily suspended operations; Qatar Airways suspended flights after Qatar closed its airspace.
  • Abu Dhabi has restarted limited operations after days of disruption, with flights running under tight oversight.
  • Dubai/Doha/Abu Dhabi are major transit gateways to Europe and the US — closures cascade into missed connections, stranded transits, and rebookings.
  • Indians are being pushed into "land-bridge" travel — seeking Saudi visas to drive to Jeddah, or Oman visas to drive to Muscat just to access flights home.
  • Airfares spiking — one-way fares to Ireland quoted up to Rs 4 lakh.
  • About 7,000 Indians stranded in Dubai since Feb 28 were flown home in the last three days, with more flights planned.
