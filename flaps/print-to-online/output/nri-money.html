NRI Cash In Indian Banks Is Rising. And It's Not Just Due To Falling ₹

A weak rupee inflates the value of money sent to India, but NRI deposits are also swelling, thanks to earnings from domestic assets

By Richa Gandhi

NRIs Put $16.2 Bn In Indian Banks In 2025

Net inflows into NRI deposits hit a record high in 2025, nearly doubling the 2023 figure and driven by both a weakening rupee and growing India-linked income streams.

Weak Rupee Magnifies Deposits

A steadily weakening rupee explains much of the divergence between rupee and dollar growth. The same deposit base appears larger in rupee terms, while the underlying foreign-currency value has grown far more modestly.

Types Of NRI Deposits

NRE
Non-Resident External
Rupee-denominated deposits of overseas earnings, fully repatriable and tax-free in India. Mainly for parking foreign income in India.
NRO
Non-Resident Ordinary
Rupee deposits of India-sourced income — rent, dividends etc. — taxable. Used for managing Indian earnings and local expenses.
FCNR(B)
Foreign Currency Non-Resident — Banks
Foreign currency deposits, fully repatriable and tax-free. For holding overseas savings in India while avoiding rupee depreciation.

Indian Assets Are Bringing In Money, Too

Earlier surges were driven by foreign-currency deposits, amplified by RBI's swap window (2013–16) before demonetisation. Today's growth is driven far more by rupee NRO and NRE balances, reflecting ongoing remittances and India-linked income rather than one-off incentives.
