# Suicide geography

- Headline: Suicide-rate outliers: a mid-sized city (Kollam) tops big-city suicide rates, while Sikkim tops states—both far above India’s average.
- Key numbers: **City suicide rate (per lakh, 2023):** Kollam 48.6; Vijayawada 38.7; Rajkot 38.6; Indore 33.1\. **State suicide rate:** Sikkim 40.2; Kerala 30.6\. **India average:** 12.7\.
- Why surprising: The surprising hook is “it’s not the usual megacities.” A clean ranking chart (rates, not counts) produces an instant outlier narrative and invites deeper questions (reporting quality vs real incidence, social factors, etc.).

## The national baseline (big number header)

- **Total suicides (2023): 171,418**
- Change vs 2022: **+0.29%** (170,924 → 171,418)
- **Suicide rate:** **12.4 (2022) → 12.3 (2023)**

_(Derived humanizing line: ~470 suicides/day, ~19.6/hour.)_

## The “rate is not count” shock (map panel)

- Rates range from **0.7 per 100,000 (Bihar)** to **49.6 per 100,000 (Andaman & Nicobar Islands)**.
- Among higher-rate states mentioned:
  - **Kerala:** **30.6**
  - **Telangana:** **27.7**
  - **Chhattisgarh:** **26.0**

**Suggested caption:**

> “India doesn’t have one suicide problem. It has dozens—some invisible unless you look at rates.”

## The caseload giants (bar chart panel: “where the numbers are”)

Among large states, CMHLP highlights:

- **Maharashtra:** **22,687**
- **Madhya Pradesh:** **19,483**
- **Tamil Nadu:** **15,662**

## The viral city outlier (spotlight panel)

From the NCRB-based reporting:

- **Kollam suicide rate:** **48.6 per lakh** (highest among cities)
- Next: **Vijayawada 38.7**, **Rajkot 38.6**, **Indore 33.1**
- Kollam suicides (count): **539**; compare with other Kerala cities (counts shown): Thrissur 501, Kannur 416, Thiruvananthapuram 410, Kozhikode 351, Kochi 337, Malappuram 142.

**Optional “count vs rate” twist (works well as a split-panel):**

- **Delhi has the highest suicide count among 53 cities: 3,131**, followed by **Bengaluru 2,370** and **Mumbai 1,415**.

## The age story (demographics panel)

- Ages **18–30 and 30–45 together account for ~66%** of suicides.
- **Child suicides (<18) increased by 5.68%** vs 2022.

## The socio-economic story (the panel that changes policy debates)

- **Daily wage earners:** **47,170** deaths (**28%**)
- **Unemployed:** **14,234** deaths (**+9%** vs 2022)
- **Farmers:** **10,780** deaths
- **Lowest income group (≤ ₹1 lakh/year): 66% of suicides**, up from **64%** in 2022.

## Causes (simple, but needs careful framing)

- Nearly **half** of suicides attributed to **family issues + illness**; **debt/poverty-linked suicides declined ~7%** (with the caveat that NCRB assigns a single cause).
