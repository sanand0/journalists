# Anaemia outlier

- Headline: India’s “anaemia outlier” is Ladakh: >90% anaemia reported across children and women—far above the national average.
- Key numbers: **NFHS-5:** India—Children (6–59m) 67.1%; Non-pregnant women (15–49) 57.2%. **Ladakh—Children 92.5%; Non-pregnant women 93.7%; Adolescent girls (15–19) 96.9%.** **Low contrast example:** Kerala—Children 39.4%; Non-pregnant women 36.5%.
- Why surprising: It’s a jaw-dropper because Ladakh is popularly associated with “clean air + fitness + mountains,” not near-universal anaemia. Also, the Kerala vs Ladakh contrast makes an irresistible bar chart.

Sources:

- https://www.pib.gov.in/PressReleasePage.aspx?PRID=1795421

## Define anaemia once (tiny footnote box)

From NFHS definitions shown in the table:

- Children 6–59 months: Hb **<11.0 g/dl**
- Non-pregnant women 15–49: Hb **<12.0 g/dl**
- Pregnant women 15–49: Hb **<11.0 g/dl**
- Adolescent boys 15–19: Hb **<13.0 g/dl**

## India’s “bad surprise” trend (make this a before/after strip)

**All-India (NFHS-4 → NFHS-5):**

- **Children anaemia:** **58.6 → 67.1**
- **Non-pregnant women:** **53.2 → 57.2**
- (NFHS-5 snapshot) **Pregnant women:** **52.2**; **All women:** **57.0**; **Adolescent girls:** **59.1**; **Adolescent boys:** **31.1**

**Suggested headline text:**

> “Anaemia got worse, not better—despite years of programmes.”

## The Ladakh shock (your centrepiece)

**Ladakh (NFHS-5):**

- **Children:** **92.5%**
- **Non-pregnant women:** **93.7%**
- **Pregnant women:** **78.1%**
- **All women:** **92.8%**
- **Adolescent girls:** **96.9%**
- **Adolescent boys:** **93.1%**

**Suggested label:**

> “In Ladakh, anaemia isn’t a minority condition. It’s close to universal.”

## A “low anaemia” comparator (to show how extreme Ladakh is)

**Kerala (NFHS-5):**

- Children **39.4%**
- Non-pregnant women **36.5%**
- Pregnant women **31.4%**
- All women **36.3%**
- Adolescent girls **32.5%**
- Adolescent boys **27.4%**

**Visual trick that works:**
A single bar for Ladakh next to Kerala and India—Ladakh towers like a skyscraper.

## Implications you can print as “what might explain this?”

These are hypotheses you can write as questions (so you’re not over-claiming):

- **Altitude & physiology:** Are haemoglobin thresholds calibrated the same way at high altitude?
- **Diet:** How much iron/folate/B12 intake is there in typical local diets across seasons?
- **Infection/inflammation:** Is anaemia of inflammation playing a larger role?
- **Service delivery:** What’s the coverage of iron supplementation, deworming, antenatal care?
- **Measurement artefacts:** Small sample, fieldwork timing, or device calibration issues could inflate extremes—worth checking the NFHS technical notes.
