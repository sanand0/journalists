# India’s hidden gold superpower

- Headline: India’s hidden gold superpower: households hold ~25,000 tonnes of gold—around 28× the RBI’s official gold reserves.
- Key numbers: **Household gold (est.):** up to 25,000 tonnes. **RBI gold holdings:** 880.2 tonnes (record). **RBI purchases:** 72.6t (2024) vs 4t (2025). **Gold share of FX reserves:** \~10% → \~16% within a year; **gold value milestone:** \~$102.4B and \~14.7% of FX reserves (Oct 2025).
- Why surprising: Everyone talks about RBI reserves, but the real “national gold stockpile” is private. The 25,000t vs 880t contrast is an instantly viral comparison (and a doorway to stories on imports, financialisation, and wealth concentration).

## The two piles (make this the hero visual)

- **Indian household gold:** “up to **25,000 tonnes**” (WGC estimate)
- **RBI gold holdings:** **880.2 tonnes** (Sept 2025 estimate using RBI weekly data, per WGC)

**Derived knockout ratio:**

- Household gold is about **28×** the RBI’s stock (25,000 / 880.2). _(derived)_

## Why this matters economically (one tight explainer box)

WGC explicitly links “mobilising” household gold to:

- reducing the need for **imported bullion**, and
- a potential positive effect on the **current account deficit**.

## RBI’s “valuation story” (add as a trend strip)

From WGC:

- In 2025, RBI added only **~4t in the first 9 months**, vs **~50t** in the same period of the prior year.
- Yet **gold share of FX reserves rose sharply (9% → 14%)**, largely due to **price gains**.

From Reuters (nice for global framing):

- RBI gold reserves value crossed **$102.365B** (week through Oct 10, 2025).
- Gold is **14.7%** of FX reserves; Reuters calls it the **highest since 1996–97** and nearly double the share from a decade ago (below 7%).
- Again: **4 tons bought in first nine months of 2025 vs 50 tons in same period 2024**.

## “Implications” panel (the story behind the shiny metal)

- **Policy tension:** Governments want to mobilise household gold; households treat it as **trust, inheritance, crisis insurance**.
- **Market tension:** A huge private stock means import policy, duty changes, and price swings can move household wealth—fast.
- **Geopolitical/portfolio angle:** Central banks (including India) increase gold share partly as diversification; valuation gains can change the reserves mix even without large purchases.
