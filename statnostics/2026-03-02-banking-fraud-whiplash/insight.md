# Banking fraud whiplash

- Headline: Banking fraud whiplash: fraud cases nearly tripled, but total money lost almost halved—because fraud shifted to many small digital hits.
- Key numbers: **All frauds:** FY2022-23 13,564 → FY2023-24 36,075\. **Amount:** ₹26,127 cr → ₹13,930 cr. **Card transaction frauds:** 6,699 (₹277 cr) → 29,082 (₹1,457 cr).
- Why surprising: “More fraud but less money” is counter-intuitive and perfect for a two-axis viz (counts vs ₹). It also reframes the story from “banks losing big” to “everyone getting nicked.”

## The two-line paradox (great as a top banner)

- **Fraud cases:** **13,564 → 36,075** (FY22-23 → FY23-24)
- **Amount involved:** **₹26,127 cr → ₹13,930 cr**

**Derived punchline metric (add as a big callout):**

- **Average ₹ per fraud case collapsed ~80%**: ~₹1.93 cr → ~₹0.39 cr per case _(derived from the above totals)_

## The “shape-shift” explanation (put as the second banner)

- **Card/Internet fraud cases:** **6,699 → 29,082**
- **Card/Internet amount:** **₹277 cr → ₹1,457 cr**
- Livemint frames it as **708% rise in online fraud cases in two years**, with **145% rise in amount duped online** to ₹1,457 cr.

**Derived “small-hit” metric (helps readers feel it):**

- Average card/internet fraud size ≈ **₹4.1 lakh → ₹5.0 lakh** _(derived)_

## “It’s still mostly a loan-fraud story in ₹ terms” (third banner)

- **Advances (loan portfolio) dominate value:** **~84% of total fraud amount** in FY23-24.
- **Advances fraud amount:** **₹11,772 cr** (FY23-24)

## “Who reports what” (fourth banner: bank groups)

- **Private-sector banks:** **24,210 frauds** (~**67%** of total frauds)
- **Public-sector banks:** highest **fraud amount**; one figure reported is **₹10,507 cr**.

## A genuinely useful nuance (tiny but powerful footnote)

- RBI flags a **significant lag between occurrence and detection** of frauds (vintage gap).

## What this lets you say (implications panel)

- **For citizens:** fraud is becoming “high-frequency, low-ticket”—a mass consumer harm story.
- **For banks:** the ₹ risk still sits heavily in **loan portfolios**, even if headlines focus on digital scams.
- **For regulators:** you need two toolkits—**cyber/UX safety** for card/internet frauds, and **credit governance** for advances.
